Build it yourself

The software is free. Build the rest.

We think everyone should be able to have a smart mirror — so the reflekt software is free forever, and this page shows you how to build one around an old tablet for about 800–1 500 kr. When you want the real-glass version, that's what we sell.

1What you need

  • A tablet — any Android tablet or iPad with a browser and a front camera. Old and unloved is perfect; it stays plugged in anyway.
  • A two-way mirror surface — the affordable options are an acrylic two-way mirror sheet (best DIY result) or mirror film applied to picture-frame glass or clear acrylic (cheapest).
  • A deep picture frame — a box/shadow frame deep enough to hide the tablet behind the sheet. IKEA-class is fine.
  • Black card or matte black paint — everything behind the glass that isn't screen must be black, or it will show as grey haze.
  • A USB charger and cable — the tablet stays on power permanently.
  • Strong double-sided tape or foam pads to hold the tablet against the back of the sheet.

2An honest word about film vs glass

We'll be straight with you, because we've built both.

Mirror film and acrylic work — you'll get a real smart mirror and it's a genuinely fun build. But film gives a slightly wavy, tinted reflection you'll notice every day, acrylic flexes and shows it, and both pass much less display light through, so the glass looks dimmer in bright rooms.

Real one-way glass is a different thing entirely — flat like a proper mirror, brighter through the glass, no waviness. It's also the single most expensive part, which is exactly why the DIY route skips it and why our kit and finished mirror use 6 mm industrial-grade one-way glass.

Our promise: the software is identical in every tier. A free build gets every feature the 9 900 kr mirror has — motion wake, phone remote, all of it. The difference you're paying for is glass and craftsmanship, nothing else.

3The build

  1. Test the layout first. Open the software on the tablet (next section), turn the brightness to maximum, and hold it behind your mirror sheet in a dark room. Decide where on the sheet you want the display.
  2. Black out the back. Cut black card to the frame's size, with a tablet-sized hole where the display goes. This is the step people skip — and the reason DIY mirrors look grey instead of black.
  3. Mount the tablet. Fix it to the back of the sheet (screen against the sheet) with foam pads or tape. Make sure the front camera isn't covered — it's what wakes the mirror when you walk up.
  4. Assemble and power. Sheet in the frame, card behind it, cable routed out the back or bottom. Plug it in.
  5. Set it up. Run through the on-screen setup: location for weather, your name, panels, colours. Turn on Motion wake so it sleeps to a plain mirror and wakes when someone's there.
Tip: a browser with a kiosk or fullscreen mode keeps the display clean, and set the tablet's screen timeout to “never” — the software's own motion sleep does the rest.

4The software

Open the reflekt mirror app in the tablet's browser — that's it, that's the install. No account, no payment, no tracking. Your settings live on the device.

  • Weather works from the first minute — just allow location or type a place.
  • Pair your phone by scanning the QR code in the mirror's settings (under Remote & phone control) to change things from the sofa.
