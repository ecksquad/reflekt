Setup guide

From box to bathroom wall

Everything you need to get a reflekt mirror running — and what every setting actually does. Most people are done with steps 1–3 in a couple of minutes; the rest is for when you want more.

1 · What you need 2 · Open the app 3 · Settings explained 4 · Business mode 5 · Mounting 6 · Troubleshooting

1What you need

  • A tablet or old phone with a working browser and front camera. An old iPad or Android tablet gathering dust is perfect — reflekt is light enough for hardware from years back.
  • A charger and cable long enough to reach where the mirror will live. The screen is on a lot, so it should stay plugged in.
  • Optional, for the full mirror effect: a frame with two-way mirror glass or acrylic to mount the tablet behind. Without it, reflekt is still a great always-on dashboard on a shelf or desk.
No account, nothing to install. reflekt is a single web page. Your settings are saved in the tablet's browser and never leave the device.

2Open the app

  1. On the tablet, open the browser and go to ecksquad.github.io/reflekt.
  2. When asked, allow location — it's used for weather and finding your nearest bus stop, nothing else.
  3. Add the page to the home screen (Share → Add to Home Screen on iPad, menu → Add to home screen on Android). Opening it from there hides the browser bars for a clean, full-screen mirror.
  4. Plug the tablet in and switch off its auto-lock (iPad: Settings → Display & Brightness → Auto-Lock → Never; Android: Display → Screen timeout). reflekt also asks the browser to keep the screen awake, but the tablet setting is the reliable backstop.

The first thing you'll see is the settings screen. Fill in the Basics, press Start mirror, and you're live. To come back to settings later, tap the small ⚙ gear in the corner of the mirror.

3Every setting, explained

Settings are grouped the same way as in the app. You only ever need Basics — everything else has sensible defaults.

Basics

ModeHome is the smart mirror. Business shows your own images or a PDF (see section 4). Stock fills the screen with live charts.
LocationTap Use my location, or type a place name (e.g. Göteborg). Used for weather.
NameOptional. Adds a personal greeting under the clock — “Good morning, Cameron”.

Clock & language

Units°C or °F for all temperatures.
Clock24h or 12h.
LanguageSvenska or English — affects dates, greetings and labels.
SecondsShow or hide seconds on the clock.

Content & panels

Centre displayWhat appears mid-screen: rotating one-liners (Swedish or English), your own image or animated GIF, a live stock chart — or nothing at all.
Stock symbol & rangeThe ticker for the centre chart (e.g. AAPL), shown over a day, week, month, year or all-time. In Stock mode you can list up to five tickers; leave a row blank to hide it.
Accent colourGold, yellow, ice, mint, rose or white — colours the date, labels and details. The clock stays white so it shines through the glass.
Bottom-left panelBus for live departures, Electricity for the hourly spot price, or off.
Electricity areaYour Swedish bidding area, SE1–SE4 (Gothenburg is SE3).
Bus departuresWorks automatically for your nearest stop once location is allowed. Advanced: point it at your own relay service or pin a specific stop ID.

Screen & motion

Keep screen onAsks the browser to prevent the screen from sleeping while the mirror runs.
Motion wakeThe mirror fades to black when no one is around and wakes when someone walks up, using the front camera. Frames are analysed on the device and never stored or sent anywhere.
Stay awake forHow long the screen stays on after the last movement — 30 seconds to 5 minutes.
Motion sensitivityLow needs a big movement; Max trips on very little. Start on Medium and adjust to your hallway.

Remote & phone control

Mirror IDA shared codeword linking your phone and the mirror. Pick something non-obvious — it works as a light password.
Remote controlSet On on the mirror tablet so it accepts pushed settings. On your phone, leave it Off and use Push settings to mirror — change anything from the sofa, no ladder required.
Remote camera viewOptionally view the mirror's camera live from another device by opening the page with ?cam=1 and the same Mirror ID. Video streams directly device-to-device.
Tip: the Eye screensaver button starts a decorative eye that watches the room — it can even be toggled remotely from your phone.

4Business mode

Business mode turns the mirror into elegant signage for a shop, salon or office — your content on black glass instead of a screen on a stick.

  • Top image — your logo or main visual. Paste a URL or choose a file from the device, and scale it from 30–100%.
  • Middle image — an optional second visual, same controls.
  • Full-screen PDF — if you set one, it replaces the images entirely. Ideal for a menu, price list or opening hours you already have as a PDF.

Switch between Home and Business any time from Basics → Mode — your Home settings are kept.

5Mounting it like a mirror

  1. Get two-way mirror glass or acrylic (also sold as “see-through mirror”). Acrylic is cheaper, lighter and safer; glass looks slightly better. Size it to your frame, not the tablet — the black page hides the tablet's edges.
  2. Build or buy a deep frame (a shadow-box frame works) so the tablet fits behind the glass.
  3. Fix the tablet centred or top-centred against the back of the glass, screen facing out. Foam tape or a 3D-printed bracket both work — just don't cover the front camera if you use motion wake.
  4. Black out the rest of the back with matte black card or paint so only the bright UI shows through.
  5. Route the cable out of the bottom of the frame and hang it near a socket.
The one rule of the mirror effect: the room in front should be brighter than the inside of the frame. Bright pixels shine through; everything else stays mirror.

6Troubleshooting

The screen keeps turning off

Set the tablet's own auto-lock to Never (see section 2) — the browser's keep-awake request can be overridden by battery savers. On Android, also disable battery optimisation for the browser. And keep it plugged in: many tablets ignore keep-awake on battery.

Motion wake doesn't trigger

Check the browser has camera permission for the site and that nothing covers the front camera (including two-way glass that's too dark in front of the lens — leave a small cutout). Then raise the sensitivity a step. If the camera permission was denied, reflekt fails safe to always-on rather than staying dark.

Weather shows nothing

The site needs a location: either allow the location permission or type a place name in Basics. If you denied the permission earlier, re-enable it in the browser's site settings and reload.

Bus departures or stocks are empty

Departures need location permission to find your nearest stop. Both departures and stock charts fetch data through a relay service — if you've entered a custom service URL under Bus departures, check it's reachable; if the field is blank, the built-in default is used.

Pushing settings from my phone does nothing

Three things must match: the mirror tablet has Remote control: On, both devices use exactly the same Mirror ID, and a relay service URL is configured. Push again and give it a few seconds — the mirror picks changes up shortly after.

I changed something and can't see the update

Force-reload the page (pull down to refresh, or hold the reload button and choose “Empty cache and hard reload” on desktop). Browsers cache aggressively for pages they show all day.

How do I get back to settings?

Tap the small ⚙ gear in the corner of the running mirror. It's dim on purpose so it disappears behind the glass.
